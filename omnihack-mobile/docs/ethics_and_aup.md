# Ethics, Law, and Acceptable Use

## Mandatory Ethics & Law Module

Participants must complete the following before accessing offensive content:

1. **Foundations of Cyber Ethics** — confidentiality, integrity, availability, responsible disclosure.
2. **Legal Frameworks** — CFAA, GDPR, regional computer misuse acts.
3. **Operational Boundaries** — authorized targets only, lab isolation boundaries.
4. **Consequences & Accountability** — incident reporting, personal liability.

### Assessment
- 20 question quiz, minimum passing score 85%.
- Attestation that all activities remain within OmniHack lab environments.

## Acceptable Use Policy

- Attacks must target OmniHack-provided lab infrastructure only.
- Probing or enumerating third-party networks is strictly forbidden.
- Credential stuffing, phishing, or social engineering outside of labs is disallowed.
- Automation tools must respect rate limits and resource quotas.
- All activity is logged; violations trigger immediate session termination and account review.

## Age & Jurisdiction

- Learners must be 18+ or have guardian consent where legally permitted.
- Users are responsible for ensuring participation is legal within their jurisdiction.

## Monitoring & Enforcement

- Network flows, container logs, and command history captured for audit.
- Automated detectors flag policy deviations (e.g., egress attempts).
- Staff reviews incidents; repeated offenses result in permanent suspension.
- Logs retained for 12 months for compliance and incident response.

## Remote Hardware Access

- Physical benches (if offered) require additional signed agreements.
- Device control limited to scheduled windows with live monitoring.

Acknowledgement of these terms is mandatory prior to lab access.
